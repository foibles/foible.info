Woven structures

When I was younger I used to often build all sorts of strange tents out of long straight lengths of hazel. A particular favourite was the torus shaped tent, it allowed large spans to be covered while maintaining a relatively high level of structural rigidity.

Inspired by my observations of the natural world I would often doodle ideas for structures. I liked the idea that rainwater could be harvested into the centre of the torus, and at the sake time a fire could be lit below. The whole arrangement resembling some sort of abstract illustration of the unification of opposites contributing to the comfort and well being of the occupants.

The inconsistencies of the materials we used wouldn't allow us to create perfectly symmetrical forms, which at the time frustrated us, but in retrospect I think it contributed its own king of charm.

I really valued the social interactive aspect we all experience while building these shelters. The experience of sitting in a newly completed structure with a team of builders was a feeling of unrivalled satisfaction.
