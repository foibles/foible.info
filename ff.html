Fabric formwork

They say if you hang a chain from either end its own weight will pull it down until it finds the perfect form to resist gravity in tension. If you where to then glue all the links of the chain together in this shape and flip it over, it would now, in its upright orientation have the perfect form to resist gravity in compression.

This principle can be carried over to surfaces by hanging a porous fabric, saturating it in a hardening liquid then flipping it over. Another approach is to use fabric as a mould for volumes of hardening liquid to produce more substantial structural elements.

I did a few experiments of my own with plaster of Paris, and another with cement. I wondered how easy it would be to make a small pagoda in this manner.
